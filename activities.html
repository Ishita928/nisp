E-Summit being the flagship event of E-Cell MSIT is held annually. It brings together the academic community, venture capitalists. new age entrepreneurs and all those passionate about entrepreneurship to common grounds. It provides early entrepreneurs the opportunity to be inclined towards entrepreneurship and take and manage bigger risks through various online events and workshops. The annual Entrepreneurship Summit, conducted by E-Cell MSIT has been one of the major and successful events at MSIT. The event includes competitions and an Internship fair. We have various guest speakers from vivid backgrounds on board who share their priceless knowledge with us.

DAY-1
  • Inauguration : The inauguration ceremony was held in the Auditorium and was hosted by Aryan Mehta (Vice President, E-Cell MSIT) who welcomed the guest of honour Dr. Javed Ahmed (Chairperson ISTE Delhi Section), chief guest Dr. N.K. Gupta, (Executive Council Member, ISTE) and Prof (Dr.) H.S. Rawat (director placement, SMES), Dr. Neeru Rathee (Convenor E-Cell MSIT). The special guests and the director gave their invaluable thoughts and inaugurated the event. The Auditorium was filled with excitement as the E-Cell team marked the commencement of the event.

  • Panel Discussion on Building Distribution through Content : The B-Plan competition was followed by a panel discussion on Building Distribution through content. In this event the special guest Bhanu Pathak (A finance content creator) guided the students on Building their own content and gave some advice on how to go about such carrier.

On 5th May 2023, E-cell MSIT organized a competition testing the brand knowledge of the participants. The session commenced at 12pm on google meet with the host Kunal who welcomed the participants and explained the rules. The competition consisted of 2 rounds both of which tested the participants' brand knowledge.

The event had a participation of around 45 students from various branches, all of whom competed to showcase their command over brand logos and taglines.

Round 1 consisted of 20 questions and was held in Typeform where participants had to guess the company from the logo/ taglines. The questions tested the participants' knowledge about various logos and taglines from various companies and brands. Teams that answered the maximum number of questions in the least time went through to round 2.

Round 2 was hosted by Manandeep and Harshika, who introduced the participants to the rules for this round. In this round the participants had to find out the company/brand from old indian tv ads. This round tested the participants memory of older famous television ads while also providing the feeling of nostalgia. After guessing the brand, the players had to message their answers and whichever team that answered the most amount of questions in the least time won the competition.

The event provided players a chance to showcase their knowledge about various brands and companies while also testing their speed and accuracy. The event concluded at 3pm with the end of round 2. In conclusion, the event was a success with great participation and was a learning experience for everyone

The educational visit to the FITT Research Centre, IIT Delhi, was organized by the E-Cell in collaboration with the Foundation for Innovation and Technology Transfer (FITT). The aim was to provide students with exposure to the entrepreneurial ecosystem at IIT Delhi and showcase the various initiatives and opportunities available for innovation and technology transfer. The visit was accompanied by 20+ students of our society and was organised under the guidance and presence of our convener, Dr. Neeru Rathi and Dr. Sudesh Pahal.

Highlights of the Session:
  • Seminar:
    • FITT organized a seminar in their auditorium, where they introduced a five-day entrepreneurial bootcamp.
    • The bootcamp is designed to assist participants in transforming their research-stage ideas into minimum viable products.
    • Participants will have the opportunity to pitch their ideas to a group of investors, providing potential funding prospects and working space in IIT Delhi.
  • Research Facility Tour:
    • The facility showcased advanced infrastructure and equipment supporting innovative research and development projects.
    • Students had the opportunity to witness diverse projects and technologies being developed at IIT Delhi.
  • Start-up Introductions:
    • Students were introduced to five tech and innovative start-ups operating within IIT Delhi.
    • The start-ups included SurfaceMoto, Tsaw, mPragati, and Dash Dynamics .
    • Students interacted with the CEOs and founders of these start-ups, gaining valuable insights into their entrepreneurial journeys and experience
    • Some start-ups also offered internship opportunities to interested students, facilitating industry-academia collaboration.

Conclusion:

The educational visit to the FITT Research Centre, IIT Delhi, was highly successful in achieving its objectives. The seminar, research facility tour, and interactions with start-ups provided students with valuable knowledge, networking opportunities, and potential internships. The visit showcased the strong entrepreneurial ecosystem at IIT Delhi and highlighted the support available for innovation and technology transfer. We express our gratitude to FITT and IIT Delhi for their warm hospitality and invaluable support in organizing this visit. Such initiatives play a crucial role in fostering an entrepreneurial mindset among students and bridging the gap between academia and industry.

The E-Cell MSIT recently organized an enlightening session on securing international internships with President of E-Cell, Drishti Pandita, as the speaker sharing her experience. The session was held on 28th August’ 23 (Monday) in the Incubation Room of MSIT and was attended by 12 members. Drawing from her experience, Drishti provided invaluable insights to the attendees into enhancing profiles and acquiring prestigious global internships.

Highlights of the Session:
  • Building Strong Foundation: She highlighted the significance of a strong foundation in the desired field. Comprehensive research enables candidates to align their skills with the expectations of international institutions.
  • Networking: Networking emerged as a crucial tool as she emphasized actively engaging with industry professionals and mentors to expand both knowledge and opportunities. LinkedIn is the proven and suggested platform for networking.
  • Resume Building: Crafting an impressive CV took center stage. She stressed the importance of a succinct CV that not only showcases academic accomplishments but also highlights extracurricular URL to Session Post: LinkedIn activities and soft skills. Real-world projects, leadership roles, and initiatives demonstrate versatility and a proactive approach.
  • Importance of Personal Statements and Cover Letters: Tailoring personal statements and cover letters for each application is vital. Adapting these documents to resonate with an institution's culture, values, and offerings significantly enhances an applicant's visibility.
  • Importance of LORs: Secure letters of recommendation from professors, mentors, or employers further establish credibility.
  • Skill Development: Candidates were advised to pursue online courses, workshops, and industry seminars to continually upgrade their expertise.

Conclusion:

The session offered a comprehensive roadmap for acquiring international internships. From thorough research and effective networking to crafting standout application documents and embracing continuous learning, Drishti Pandita's experience serves as a guiding light. As MSIT's E-Cell endeavours to nurture future global leaders, her insights provide vital guidance for attaining international internships and pushing one's boundaries.

The Incubation Department of Ecell embarked on a remarkable journey to explore the world of “Brand Renaissance: A Rebranding Workshop”. The Workshop organised at the Masters' Union Campus, DLF Cyber Park, was conducted with great professionalism and care which provided the young entrepreneurs with an extraordinary and insightful experience on “Brand Renaissance”.

Highlights of the Session:
  • The campus tour: The workshop began with getting the attendees accustomed to the campus to create a sense of familiarity and for the smooth functioning of the events that followed.
  • Introduction to "Brand Renaissance": The first segment of the workshop began with the introduction of the speaker, Mr Akhil Chopra, Senior Creative Director at Schbang who introduced to everyone briefly about Rebranding and why it plays an important role in today's industry
  • Why rebranding: He explained why rebranding was being implemented in today's market, like the repositioning of a brand/change in mission and vision of the brand or to contemporise and undust the brand in an attempt to adapt to the modern markets and newer customer base. Rebranding is also possible in the case of mergers or in the case of a new leadership as in the case of Burberry.
  • How to Rebrand: It was further explained how the rebranding process is to start, that is to primarily understand the need and the purpose for the rebranding. The next step involves the required changes as accepted by the involved company which could include a change in the logo, the brand personality, digital architecture, typography etc. The first segment was then brought to a close for a lunch break.
  • Problem analysis: In the next half, after the lunch, the attendees were instructed to form teams of 9 and given a problem statement to Rebrand patanjali. This segment tested the participants on their understanding of rebranding and its implementation and were instructed to present their solution within a 2hr time limit.
  • The Result: After every team pitched in their solutions, the speaker Mr Akhil announced the results and our Incubation dept team emerged as the winner with one of the most creative and successful rebranding propagandas. The team members were then rewarded with Amazon gift vouchers as a show of appreciation to their brilliant solution to the problem.

Conclusion:

The workshop offered a transformative and insightful experience on "Brand Renaissance", equipping the involved with the knowledge and tools required to elevate their understanding on Rebranding. From the excellent program management to fun interactions, it provided it all. We are thankful to Masters' Union for extending such a gracious invitation to their event and look forward to future collaborations with the team as discussed with Mrs. Jigyasa Malhotra, the event and community manager at Masters' Union

Faculty Development Program--GST using Tally

June 8, 2020 Tally Education Pvt. Ltd. organized a Faculty Development Program that covered concepts of Goods and Services Tax (GST) and its Business Applications using Tally. This FDP was brought and introduced to MSIT by eCell. The webinar had 350 attendees from over 8 various colleges.

It is a group company and an education initiative of Tally Solutions, the makers of the popular Tally software. It is the only entity authorised to issue certifications on Computerized Accounting and on Tally. It has been incorporated with a vision to enable businesses to manage their manpower requirements by offering simple and easy solutions to find suitable skilled resources for their businesses.

The webinar was constructed in a continuous set up to retain the maximum and foster the proficiency in the concepts of GST. In the beginning, fundamentals of GST and its practical was discussed, following indirect taxation revolution since our independence. It pondered the fact that GST is one tax, one nation, one market. Later on, the tax structure outlining taxes or goods subsumed/omitted under GST were clarified. Determination of GST (CGST, SGST, IGST) and the comparison of tax scenario under pre-GST and GST were explained after.

The Faculty Development Program was a 3 hour live stream conducted by Mr Bijay from Tally Education Pvt. Ltd. Which included a session where the attendees were asked to clear their doubts, and to give in their suggestions and reviews. This webinar was summed up with a discussion on the existing dealer registrations, conditions and composition schemes. eCell MSIT felt a need for such an event to provide the budding entrepreneurs a fair chance and opportunity to get to know the legal stuff which plays a significant role in entrepreneurship.

To get an edge over and an exposure to non-technical sector, eCell has successfully assessed the FDP- GST using Tally. The event was concluded at 1:00 pm on the same day.
